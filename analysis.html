How these two branding approaches differ in positioning, perception, and consumer demographics.

Effects

Dependent Variables

The consumer outcomes influenced by branding strategies — measuring loyalty, perception, and behavior.

Category Retention

The proportion of consumers who continue consuming soft drinks rather than shifting to alternative beverages like water or juice.

Perceived Health Halo

The belief that "Diet" or "Zero" drinks are healthier choices, regardless of the actual nutritional content or use of artificial sweeteners.

Brand Loyalty

The strength of consumer attachment to a specific brand, measured by repeat purchases and emotional connection.

Sugar Craving Persistence

The extent to which consumers continue craving sweet tastes after consuming artificially sweetened beverages.

"Consumers may infer permission to consume more or reduce other healthful behaviors when a product is labeled as 'diet' or 'zero' — the classic health halo phenomenon."
Strategy

Independent Variables

The strategic levers used by brands to influence consumer perception and behavior.

Zero/diet beverages are a hybrid phenomenon — they carry genuine potential to reduce sugar intake at scale but are simultaneously constructed and promoted in ways that prioritize brand continuity.

— Evidence Synthesis, Section 7

Labeling Nomenclature

The deliberate use of names such as "Diet" (associated with health and calorie control) or "Zero Sugar" (associated with strength and performance) to influence consumer mindset.

Sensory Replication

The extent to which the beverage mimics the flavor, mouthfeel, and overall sensory experience of the original sugary drink.

Marketing Narrative

The thematic focus of promotional campaigns — whether emphasizing health benefits, real taste, or lifestyle freedom.

Price Parity

The strategy of aligning the price of zero-calorie variants with regular products to reduce economic hesitation and encourage switching.
