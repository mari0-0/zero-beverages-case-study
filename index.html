Research Problem

The Wellness Paradox

In a wellness-driven market, do "Zero" and "Diet" beverages genuinely promote healthier consumer choices and awareness, or are they strategic branding tools designed to retain customer loyalty despite ongoing health controversies?

01

Health Perception

How do consumers interpret and differentiate health messages associated with "Zero" versus "Diet" beverage labels? We examine the perceived health meaning and semantic framing.

02

Marketing Influence

What effects do different marketing narratives — health benefits, taste authenticity, lifestyle positioning — have on consumers' perception of these products as health-oriented?

03

Category Retention

Do consumers of "Zero" and "Diet" beverages continue soft drink consumption, or do they switch to non-carbonated or naturally healthier alternatives?

1980s–1990s
The Rise of "Diet" Branding

Diet beverages emerge as weight-management tools, driven by calorie-counting culture and growing concern about sugar consumption.

2000s
Health Consciousness Expands

Scientific attention to sugar-related metabolic harms grows. Regulatory bodies begin considering sugar taxes and public-health campaigns.

2005–2015
"Zero" Rebrand Emerges

Brands shift from "diet" to "zero" — targeting a broader audience and shedding the diet-culture stigma with modern, masculine-coded marketing.

2015–Present
Wellness Culture Integration

Zero-sugar products become lifestyle products. Clean labels, minimalist packaging, and endorsements blur the line between health choice and brand strategy.
